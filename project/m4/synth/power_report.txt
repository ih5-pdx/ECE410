Cell-Count Based Estimate (sky130_fd_sc_hd, TT corner, 1.8V, 25°C)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Static (leakage) power:
  sky130_fd_sc_hd leakage:      ~1 nW/cell (typical)
  Total cells (M4):              2,097
  Estimated leakage power:      ~2.1 µW

Dynamic power (α·C·V²·f model):
  α (activity factor):           0.10  (10% of FFs switch per MAC cycle;
                                         AXI register file switches rarely)
  C (per-cell capacitance):      ~2 fF (sky130_fd_sc_hd average)
  V:                             1.8 V
  f:                             100 MHz
  Dynamic ≈ 0.10 × 2097 × 2e-15 × 1.8² × 100e6
           ≈ 0.10 × 2097 × 3.24e-7
           ≈ ~135 nW

Total estimated power (leakage + dynamic):  ~2.1 µW + 0.135 µW ≈ ~2.2 µW

Context and Caveats
--------------------
1. This estimate is derived from cell count alone without parasitic capacitance
   from routing, which typically adds 30–50% to the switching power.
2. The activity factor α=0.10 is a conservative estimate for the MAC datapath
   during active computation. The AXI slave registers toggle much less frequently
   (only during host transactions).
3. A routed sky130 design with full OpenROAD power analysis typically reports
   1.5–3× higher dynamic power than this estimate due to wire capacitance.
4. The dominant power consumer is the compute_core multiplier (525 XOR cells
   active every MAC cycle). A more accurate estimate for this block alone:
     P_mult ≈ 0.30 × 525 × 2e-15 × 3.24 × 1e8 ≈ ~102 nW
