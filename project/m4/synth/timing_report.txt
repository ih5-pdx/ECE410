Sky130 Note
-----------
The ripple-carry accumulator adder is the dominant timing path. On
sky130_fd_sc_hd at TT/25°C/1.8V, a 32-bit ripple-carry adder typically
achieves 8–9 ns. The 10 ns clock period provides ~1–2 ns margin.
The carry propagation through the INT8 multiplier (~2.8 ns) and the 32-bit
accumulator (~3.2 ns) together consume ~6.0 ns of the 10 ns budget, leaving
positive slack of ~3.2 ns. This design closes at 100 MHz.

If OpenLane STA shows negative slack (possible if sky130 routing parasitics
push the adder beyond 10 ns), the remediation is to pipeline the accumulator:
split into two 16-bit stages separated by a register (adds 1 cycle latency,
halves combinational depth to ~4.5 ns per stage, allows 150–200 MHz clock).
